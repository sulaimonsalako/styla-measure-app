# STYLA Measure: 5-Pillar Content Strategy

**Core Philosophy:** Pain-Avoidance Marketing. We never just post pictures of clothes. We post the problem (returns, wasted money, bad fits) and immediately offer the cure (styla.ca).

## 1. The "Pain Architecture" Carousels (Core Educational)
4-slide visual frameworks calling out a specific problem (The Hook), visualizing the frustration (The Pain), demonstrating the STYLA UI (The Fix), and driving traffic to styla.ca.

## 2. Short-Form Text Posts (Core Storytelling)
Punchy, text-only posts using the A.V.A Framework (Acknowledge, Validate, Action) to quickly tell a relatable shopping story and drop the link.

## 3. "Deal Drops" + Sizing Utility (Core Traffic Driver)
Curated fashion sales (e.g., "Zara 40% off sale today") paired with the prompt: "Don't know your Zara size? Use STYLA to decode your measurements before the sale ends."

## 4. Relatable Memes (Core Engagement)
Highly shareable, comic-style or pop-culture memes that strictly focus on the ridiculousness of online sizing, the pain of the post office return line, or the heartbreak of an item not fitting. This lowers the barrier to entry and generates organic shares/tags.

## 5. Ad-Hoc / Founder Content (Core Authenticity)
Generic, behind-the-scenes, or spontaneous content you come up with from time to time. This could be a quick thought on the fashion industry, a screen-recording of a new feature you just built, or an impromptu styling tip. This keeps the brand feeling human and founder-led.
