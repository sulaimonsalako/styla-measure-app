Most brands don't design plus-size clothes. They just multiply the math.

Ever notice how some plus-size clothing fits weirdly? The armholes sag, the neckline is too wide, or the proportions just feel... off.

Here's why: most fashion brands design a garment on a size 8 fit model, and then use a grading formula to scale it up to a 2X or 3X. They assume a plus-size body is just a larger, uniform version of a straight-size body.

It’s not. Curvier bodies have different weight distributions, bust shapes, and movement patterns. 

If you want clothing that is actually designed for your body, look for brands that practice individual size grading:

1. Universal Standard: They design and fit every single size (from 00 to 40) on a real person of that size. Plus, their "Fit Liberty" program lets you swap sizes for free if your body changes.
2. Eloquii: Specialize in plus-fashion with options like the "Viola" fit, designed for bodies where hips are 1-2 sizes larger than the bust.
3. Wray NYC: Beautiful, artistic clothes graded on diverse body shapes up to 6XL.

When brands actually design for your shape, the fit is spectacular. But because their grading is so precise, guessing your size from a standard chart won't work.
