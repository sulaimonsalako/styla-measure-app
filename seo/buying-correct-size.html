How to Buy the Correct Clothing Size Online (Every Single Time)

Published: May 2026 By Styla Fit Team Read time: 5 min

Buying clothing online has become a standard convenience, but it comes with a major, frustrating caveat: sizing is completely inconsistent. A size Medium in Zara can fit tighter than a size Small in H&M, while designer brands often run on obscure sizing scales. This inconsistency is driven by "vanity sizing" and varying design fits.

To avoid spending money on return shipping fees and entering the frustrating loop of returns, you must develop a systematic way to measure yourself and match it to store charts. Here is your ultimate sizing checklist.

1. Get Your Exact Body Measurements

Do not guess your measurements based on your height and weight. Use a soft, flexible tailor's tape measure. If you do not have one, you can wrap a phone charging cable or string around yourself, mark the lengths, and lay it against a metal tape measure.

The Core Measurements to Take:

  • Chest / Bust: Wrap the tape measure under your armpits and around the fullest part of your chest. Keep the tape parallel to the floor and do not puff out your chest.
  • Waist: Measure around your natural waistline (usually right above your belly button, or where your torso creases when bending to the side). Do not suck in your stomach.
  • Hips: Stand with your feet together and measure around the widest point of your glutes/upper thighs.
  • Inseam: For pants, measure from your crotch down to the ankle bone along the inside of your leg.

📏 Pro Tip: Use a Mirror

Make sure the tape is flat and not twisted across your back. If the tape is angled or drooping, it will artificially inflate the measurements by 1 to 2 inches.

2. Learn to Read Flat Size Charts

Many online retailers post "flat measurements" rather than body measurements. Flat measurements represent the dimensions of the physical clothing laid out on a table.

To decode these tables, simply double the width numbers to get the total circumference:

  • If a shirt's flat size chart lists a Chest Width of 20 inches, the circumference is 40 inches. If your chest body measurement is 38 inches, this shirt will fit you with 2 inches of comfortable breathing room.
  • If a pair of jeans lists a Waist of 16 inches, the flat waist circumference is 32 inches.

3. Understand Silhouette & Stretch

Once you match your numbers to a chart, think about how the fabric behaves and the designer's intent:

  • Fit Style (Oversized, Relaxed, Slim): An "oversized" t-shirt is designed to measure much larger than your body. If you want a standard fit, order one size down. If a shirt is "slim-fit", order your standard size if you are lean, or size up for comfort.
  • Elastane / Spandex Content: Activewear and denim with spandex will stretch horizontal. Choose your exact body size or slightly smaller for a snug fit. Non-stretch items (heavy cotton canvas, denim) require at least 1.5 inches of breathing room.
